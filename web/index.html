01 — Key Findings

Where the money goes.
And where it disappears.

Five data-driven findings that directly answer the central business question.

finding_01
High discounts destroy profit
Orders with discounts above 40% generated a total loss of $100,030. The swing from no-discount to high-discount orders is a $171 difference in average profit per order — with no volume compensation.
High discount avg: -$105/order
finding_02
Furniture has a 2.6% margin
Despite $754K in sales — nearly as much as Technology — Furniture generated only $19,730 in profit. Tables sub-category alone lost $17,753. The category is essentially a revenue illusion.
2.6% vs 17.5% in Technology
finding_03
Central region underperforms
The Central region has a 7.9% margin despite $503K in sales. That's nearly half the West region's 15.0% margin on similar volume — a structural issue likely driven by discount policies.
7.9% margin vs 15.0% in West
finding_04
Strong Q4 seasonality every year
Sep–Dec consistently outperforms Jan–Feb across all four years in the dataset. Q4 accounts for a disproportionate share of annual profit, making inventory planning critical for sustained performance.
Consistent 4-year pattern
finding_05
Technology leads on every metric
17.5% margin on $840K in sales. Copiers ($56K profit) and Phones ($45K profit) are the top sub-categories. Technology is the engine — every marketing dollar here yields the highest return.
$146K total profit

02 — SQL Analysis

Eight phases.
One clean script.

All analysis performed in MySQL 8.0, organized into phases from data cleaning through to production-ready views.

Phase 01

Data Cleaning

Null check on Sales and Profit (zero found). Detected 2 genuine full-row duplicates via Group By approach excluding Row_ID. Final table: 10,192 rows.

Phase 02

Basic Metrics

Baseline KPIs: 5,111 unique orders, $2.33M total sales, $292K profit, 12.6% overall margin.

Phase 03

Time-Series Analysis

Monthly sales and profit trends 2023–2026. YoY growth using LAG() window function. Revealed consistent Q4 seasonality.

Phase 04

Region Analysis

Sales, profit, and order count by region using WITH ROLLUP for automatic subtotals. Identified Central as the underperformer.

Phase 05

Category & Sub-Category

Profit margin by category and sub-category. Identified Tables (-$17,753), Bookcases (-$3,632), and Supplies (-$1,171) as loss-makers.

Phase 06

Discount Impact

Raw discount-profit correlation then banded into 4 groups. Proved discounts above 20% consistently produce negative returns.

Phase 07

Customer RFM

Recency, Frequency, Monetary analysis per customer. Top customers exceed $20K in lifetime value. 804 unique customers in dataset.

Phase 08

Shipping Performance

Average shipping days, order count, and sales by ship mode. Standard Class handles 60% of all orders at $1.38M in sales.

04 — Recommendations

What the data says
leadership should do.

Five actionable recommendations derived directly from the analysis findings.

🚫

Cap discounts at 20%

Discounts above this threshold consistently produce negative average profit. No volume justification found in the data.

🪑

Review Tables pricing

$17,753 total loss on Tables sub-category. Either reprice, reduce discounting, or discontinue the worst-performing SKUs.

🗺️

Audit Central region

7.9% margin is nearly half the West's 15.0%. Investigate discount policies in Central and align with top-performing region practices.

💻

Prioritize Technology

17.5% margin on $840K in sales. Every marketing dollar invested in Technology yields the highest return in the portfolio.

📅

Plan Q4 inventory early

Consistent Sep–Dec demand spikes across all 4 years. Begin inventory and staffing planning 60 days before Q4 each year.
